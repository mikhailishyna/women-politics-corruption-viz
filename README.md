# Women in Politics, Corruption & Quality of Life
### Data Visualization Portfolio | Tableau Public

> *Does women's access to political power correlate with lower corruption and a better quality of life?*

This portfolio explores the relationship between women's political representation, corruption levels, and population well-being across countries. Five interconnected Tableau dashboards guide the viewer from a global overview of political gender gaps to a deeper look at corruption trends and their impact on happiness and life expectancy.

**[→ View Live Portfolio](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/valeriia.mikhailishyna/viz/PortfolioProject_17662523330830/GlobalCPIdynamics20122024)

## Visualizations

| # | Title | Chart Types | Key Question |
|---|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | Women's Representation in National Parliaments | Choropleth map + bar chart | How is political gender representation distributed globally? |
| 2 | Countries with Largest CPI Changes (2012–2024) | Slope graph + divergent bar chart | Which countries improved or worsened in corruption? |
| 3 | Global CPI Dynamics (2012–2024) | Line chart + scatter plot | Are changes in corruption systemic or isolated? |
| 4 | Corruption Impact on Happiness & Life Expectancy | Dual scatter plots | Does less corruption mean longer, happier lives? |
| 5 | Women in Politics vs. Corruption | Small multiples scatter plot + trend lines | Do countries with more women in parliament have less corruption? |

---

## Key Findings

- Most countries remain well below 30% women's representation in parliament, though the share is slowly increasing
- Global average corruption (CPI) has stayed nearly flat since 2012 — but country-level changes vary dramatically
- Countries with lower corruption consistently show higher happiness scores and longer life expectancy
- **No clear correlation** was found between women's political representation and corruption levels — other factors appear to be stronger drivers

## Design Highlights

- Interactive **year parameter** on the map to track representation changes over time
- **Top N parameter** to control how many countries are displayed in rankings
- Diagonal **reference line** on scatter plots to instantly distinguish improvement vs. deterioration
- Consistent **color coding**: red = worsened, light blue = improved, dark blue = unchanged
